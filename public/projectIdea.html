Testing

Our testing will be completed in alpha and beta stages before being rolled out to the public. Throughout our alpha testing stage, we will be relying on our groups immediate family members to complete testing on our platform. The number we are aiming for will be 10-20 users. We will be asking that they test every aspect of our application including registering an account, logging in, using the location tools, testing the community live chat feature, and testing to ensure all article links are active and always refreshing with the latest ones.

Once alpha testing is complete and any bugs found are rectified, we will move into the beta phase of our testing. This will include rolling it out to a larger amount of people, however it will only be extended families and friends of the creators. The number of testers we will be looking for at this stage will be between 50-100. With a larger number of users accessing the platform, it will allow us to pressure test it and ensure that any bugs that exist are found.

Finding bugs in testing is not a negative, it is a positive and a great way to measure success. The more bugs that are found during testing will help reduce the number of bugs found during the launch and rollout of our platform.

Group Processes and Communication

Communication is a key factor in determining the success and functionality of any group project. Our group would hold weekly meetings on Sunday and Thursday for the first 2 weeks of the project. This was later changed to Monday and Wednesday due to group membersâ€™ work commitments and availability. These meetings would be held over Microsoft Teams, with one team member in charge of taking the minutes of the meeting. The first meeting every week would be used to discuss project ideas, brainstorming which task would need to be completed for the week and ensuring everyone was on track at the time. The second meeting each week would be used as a progress check, to make sure every team member was completing their task within the given timeframe, whilst continuing to discuss possible tasks and roles for the development of the application.

Throughout the week, team members will regularly communicate through the Microsoft Teams application, whether it be on their computer/laptop or on their phone. Communication outside of the meetings will generally be for questions and progress updates. On the off chance a team member is unavailable to attend the meetings, they will watch the recorded meeting on Microsoft Teams, and provide input on their current progress. If a team member does not respond to communications, other avenues of communication will be used. The team has a Discord chat with the 6 members of the group, which will be used as an alternative if there is no response. After repeated messaging attempts being unsuccessful, group members who participated in the group meetings will be allocated additional tasks. If the group member who does not respond is unable to complete parts of the assessment, it will not be a detriment to the group.
